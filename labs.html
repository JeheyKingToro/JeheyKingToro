Designing Controllers

  • Digital Control and Pendulum Balancing Project

    It was a group work where we designed and implemented digital controllers using MATLAB, Simulink, and QUARC for a servo-driven rotary pendulum system. The project involved modelling continuous and discrete-time control systems with zero-order hold, analysing step responses, and designing lead compensators for improved transient performance. A full state-space model of the rotary pendulum was derived using Euler–Lagrange dynamics, linearised, and validated through controllability analysis. State-feedback gains were calculated using pole placement to meet specified performance criteria (overshoot and settling time), and the controller was successfully deployed on Quanser QUBE-Servo 3 hardware to achieve real-time pendulum balancing. The effects of sampling time on digital control performance were also analysed and evaluated.

  • Designing PID Controllers

    Completed a control systems assignment focused on designing and tuning PID and lead/lag controllers using MATLAB for robotic and aircraft control applications. The work involved modelling dynamic systems, selecting controller parameters to meet performance requirements such as low overshoot, fast settling time, and minimal steady-state error, and analysing system responses through simulation. Multiple controller designs were evaluated and compared to verify stability, damping, and tracking performance, demonstrating practical controller design skills for real-world robotic and aerospace systems.

  • Magnetic Levitation (MATLAB & Control Systems)

    Analysed and modelled a nonlinear magnetic levitation (Maglev) system using MATLAB. Linearised the nonlinear dynamics about an equilibrium point, derived the plant transfer function, and evaluated system stability using root locus techniques. Designed and compared P, PD, and PID controllers through simulation and experimental testing, analysing step responses, stability, accuracy, and transient performance. Demonstrated practical understanding of controller effects on nonlinear systems and real-world validation through laboratory experiments.

PLC Programming

  • What is a PLC (Programmable Logic Controller)?

    A PLC is a specialised industrial computer used to control machines and automated processes. It reads inputs from sensors and switches, processes them using a control program, and then controls outputs such as motors, valves, and actuators. PLCs are designed to be reliable, fast, and robust, making them ideal for use in factories, production lines, and automated systems like pick-and-place mechanisms.

    Pick-and-Place PLC Programming

    The PLC program controls a pick-and-place mechanism using a step-by-step sequence. It starts when the operator presses the start button, moves the arm to the pick position, lowers it to grab the object using a vacuum gripper, then lifts and moves the object to the place position. After releasing the object, the system returns to its home position and waits for the next cycle. Sensors are used at each stage to confirm positions, ensuring safe, accurate, and reliable operation.
